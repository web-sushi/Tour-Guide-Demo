About Your Guide

Konnichiwa! I'm Ken, a licensed private tour guide and Tokyo native with over 8 years of experience sharing my city with travelers from around the world. Born and raised in Tokyo, I've watched this incredible city evolve while maintaining its deep cultural roots.

My passion is connecting visitors with the authentic Tokyo, the hidden temples tucked away in residential neighborhoods, the family-run restaurants serving recipes passed down for generations, and the local stories that bring each district to life. I speak fluent English and Japanese, and I'm certified by the Japan National Tourism Organization.

What sets my tours apart? Flexibility. Every tour is customized to your interests, pace, and curiosity. Whether you're a foodie wanting to explore the best ramen shops, a history buff fascinated by samurai culture, or someone simply wanting to experience daily life in Tokyo, I'll craft an itinerary that feels personal and meaningful.

I believe the best travel experiences happen when you step off the beaten path. Let me show you the Tokyo I know and love, the one that exists beyond the guidebooks and Instagram posts.

Walk through Nakamise Shopping Street, discover hidden shrines, and learn about traditional crafts. Frequently Asked Questions

How do I book a tour?

Simply click the "Book a Tour" button anywhere on the site, fill out the inquiry form with your preferred date, group size, and interests, and I'll get back to you within 24 hours to confirm details and answer any questions.

What's included in the tour price?

The tour price includes my services as your guide, route planning, cultural insights, and local recommendations. Food, drinks, entrance fees, and transportation costs are not included unless specified. I'll always let you know about any additional costs beforehand.

Can you accommodate dietary restrictions?

Absolutely! Whether you're vegetarian, vegan, have food allergies, or follow halal/kosher diets, I can adjust the tour to include suitable dining options. Just mention your dietary needs when booking.

What if it rains?

Tours operate rain or shine! Tokyo has covered areas and indoor attractions we can visit. If the weather is severe, we can reschedule or adjust the itinerary. I'll keep you informed and flexible.

Do you offer tours in languages other than English?

Yes! I'm fluent in English and Japanese. For other languages, I can arrange a translator or provide written materials in advance. Please let me know your language preference when booking.

What should I bring on the tour?

Comfortable walking shoes are essential! Also bring a water bottle, camera, cash (many small shops don't accept cards), and an umbrella during rainy season. I'll send a detailed list after booking.
